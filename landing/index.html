Built for sensitive camera roll realities.

IDs, finance screenshots, medical photos, and “why did I screenshot this” receipts don’t belong in the same accidental backup path as memes. LumaVault is for the subset of your life you want to isolate—without turning your phone into a settings puzzle.

Encrypt on-device

Vaulted photos and videos are encrypted on your phone. This is a consumer vault product—not a promise of absolute security, but a concrete design: keys and ciphertext handled locally.

Lock that fits real life

Biometric and PIN workflows help keep the vault behind a barrier that still feels usable for frequent access.

Local intelligence, optional power-ups

Helpers like cleanup, classification, sensitive review, and privacy redaction are oriented around on-device analysis—so “smart” doesn’t default to “upload everything.”

Backup without silent mirrors

Encrypted backup/restore is designed to be intentional: recovery when you want it—not a surprise thumbnail sync story.

Premium when you’re ready

Plain-English privacy posture

FAQ

Short answers you can stand behind pre-launch. Adjust to match your final Play Data safety section.

Does LumaVault upload my photos to the cloud?

LumaVault is designed around an offline-first vault: your vaulted media is encrypted on-device and is not a cloud photo service. Some non-media flows (like subscription validation through Google Play / RevenueCat) are normal for apps and are separate from bulk photo uploads.

Is this “unhackable” or “military-grade”?

No serious product should claim that. LumaVault focuses on practical protection: local encryption, explicit exports, and UX that reduces common foot-guns—while acknowledging real-world device risks (malware, physical access, backups you enabled elsewhere).
